Arroz Caldo

Arroz Caldo is a comforting Filipino rice porridge that is often enjoyed as a warm and nourishing meal, especially during cooler weather or when you're feeling under the weather. Made with glutinous rice, this hearty dish is simmered in a rich broth flavored with ginger, garlic, and onion, resulting in a creamy and slightly thick consistency. Traditionally, chicken is added, making it a filling source of protein, but variations can include other meats or seafood. Arroz Caldo is typically garnished with green onions, fried garlic, and a squeeze of calamansi (Filipino lime) for an extra burst of flavor. Often served with a side of soy sauce and hard-boiled eggs, this dish is a beloved comfort food that showcases the heartwarming flavors of Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice (or regular rice)
  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces, bone-in for more flavor)
  • 6 cups chicken broth (or water)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1-inch piece ginger (sliced)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (optional)
  • 2 green onions (chopped)
  • Fried garlic (for topping))
  • Hard-boiled eggs (optional)
  • Calamansi or lemon (for serving)

Instructions:
  1. Sauté the Aromatics:
    • In a large pot, heat cooking oil over medium heat.
    • Add the sliced onion, minced garlic, and ginger. Sauté until fragrant and the onions are translucent.
  2. Add the Chicken:
    • Add the chicken pieces to the pot. Cook until lightly browned on all sides.
    • If using, stir in the fish sauce and cook for another minute.
  3. Add the Rice and Broth:
    • Add the glutinous rice and stir to coat it in the oil and aromatics.
    • Pour in the chicken broth (or water) and bring to a boil.
    • Reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for about 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice is soft and the porridge reaches your desired consistency.
  4. Season:
    • Taste and season with salt and pepper as needed.
  5. Serve:
    • Ladle the arroz caldo into bowls.
    • Garnish with chopped green onions, fried garlic, and a hard-boiled egg if desired.
    • Serve with calamansi or lemon on the side for a citrusy kick.
  6. Enjoy your Arroz Caldo! It’s a delicious and warming breakfast dish, perfect for any day, especially during cooler weather.
