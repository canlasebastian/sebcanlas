Tapsilog

Tapsilog is a popular Filipino breakfast dish that combines three essential components: tapa (cured beef), sinangag (garlic fried rice), and itlog (fried egg). The tapa is marinated in a savory blend of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices, then pan-fried to create a tender and flavorful beef dish. The garlic fried rice is typically cooked with leftover rice, infused with aromatic garlic, making it a delicious complement to the rich flavors of the tapa. Tapsilog is often served with a side of pickled vegetables or atchara for a refreshing contrast. This hearty meal is not just for breakfast; it’s enjoyed any time of the day, making it a beloved staple in Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef (sirloin or flank, thinly sliced)
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions:
  1. Marinate the Beef:
    • In a bowl, combine soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, brown sugar, black pepper, and salt.
    • Add the sliced beef, ensuring it's well coated with the marinade.
    • Cover and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight for better flavor.
  2. Cook the Tapa:
    • Heat a pan over medium heat and add a little cooking oil.
    • Remove the beef from the marinade (reserve the marinade) and cook in the pan until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
    • If desired, pour in the reserved marinade and let it simmer for a few minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
    • Set aside.
  3. Prepare the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):
    • In the same pan, add more oil if needed and sauté the minced garlic until golden brown.
    • Add the cooked rice, breaking any clumps. Stir-fry until heated through.
    • Season with salt to taste. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Fry the Eggs:
    • In a separate pan, heat a bit of oil over medium heat.
    • Crack the eggs into the pan and fry until the whites are set and the yolks are cooked to your preference. (Sunny-side up is traditional.)
  5. Optional Creamy Version:
    • If you prefer a creamy sisig, stir in 2 tablespoons of mayonnaise and mix well. This step is optional but adds a nice creamy texture.
  6. Assemble the Tapsilog:
    • On a plate, serve a portion of garlic fried rice, topped with the cooked tapa and a fried egg.
    • Optionally, you can serve with sliced tomatoes or a side of vinegar for dipping.
  7. Enjoy your Tapsilog! This dish is not just delicious but also a great way to start your day.
