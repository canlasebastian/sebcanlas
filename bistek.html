Bistek Tagalog

Bistek Tagalog, also known as Filipino Beef Steak, is a savory dish that highlights the rich flavors of marinated beef. Thinly sliced beef is marinated in soy sauce, calamansi juice (or lemon), garlic, and black pepper, then pan-fried until tender and flavorful. The dish is traditionally topped with caramelized onions, adding a sweet and aromatic element that complements the savory meat. Often served with steamed rice, Bistek Tagalog is a comforting and satisfying meal that embodies the heart of Filipino home cooking. Its simple yet delicious preparation makes it a favorite for family gatherings and everyday meals alike.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef sirloin or round, sliced thinly
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • 1 large onion, sliced into rings
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp sugar (optional)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Steamed rice, for serving

Instructions:
  1. Marinate the Beef:
    • In a bowl, combine the sliced beef, soy sauce, calamansi juice, minced garlic, and ground black pepper. Mix well to ensure the beef is evenly coated with the marinade.
    • Cover the bowl and let the beef marinate for at least 1 hour in the refrigerator (overnight for best results). This step will infuse the beef with the savory, tangy flavors of the marinade.
  2. Sauté the Onions:
    • In a large pan or skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of cooking oil over medium heat
    • Add the onion rings and sauté for about 2-3 minutes until they are soft and slightly translucent. Remove the onions from the pan and set them aside for later.
  3. Cook the Beef
    • In the same pan, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of cooking oil and heat over medium-high heat.
    • Add the marinated beef slices in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Sear the beef for 1-2 minutes on each side or until browned. You want to quickly brown the meat to lock in the flavor. Do not overcook as the beef will become tough.
    • Once all the beef is browned, set it aside on a plate.
  4. Simmer the Sauce:
    • In the same pan, pour in the marinade from the beef and add 1/2 cup of water. Stir to scrape up any bits stuck to the pan from the beef (this adds extra flavor).
    • Bring the sauce to a boil, then lower the heat to a simmer. Let it cook for about 5 minutes to slightly reduce the liquid and intensify the flavors.
    • Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning. If you want a bit of sweetness, add 1/2 tsp of sugar. You can also add more calamansi juice for additional tanginess.
  5. Combine the Beef and Onions:
    • Add the browned beef back into the pan with the simmering sauce. Stir well to coat the beef in the sauce.
    • Simmer for another 5-7 minutes, allowing the beef to absorb the flavors of the sauce.
  6. Serve:
    • Turn off the heat and transfer the Bistek Tagalog to a serving dish. 14. Top the beef with the sautéed onion rings for a beautiful presentation.
    • Serve with hot steamed rice to enjoy the savory and tangy sauce.
  7. Enjoy your Bistek Tagalog! This Filipino classic is a delightful combination of savory, tangy, and slightly sweet flavors that make for a perfect meal any day of the week.
